Chapter One

The work that shaped everything.

Gigi Bratakos has spent over two decades as an Alzheimer's and dementia specialist — not from the outside looking in, but from every angle that matters. As the owner and operator of Residential Care Facilities for the Elderly (RCFEs), she built environments where people with dementia could live with dignity, safety, and genuine warmth.

But Gigi didn't just run facilities — she worked in them. As a caregiver, a problem-solver, an advocate. She knows what a facility should feel like when it's doing its job right, and she knows the red flags that families often miss until it's too late.

With a Bachelor's in Psychology from UC San Diego and credentials as a Registered Behavior Technician (RBT), Gigi brings formal training in human behavior to every conversation — understanding not just the medical progression of dementia, but the emotional, behavioral, and relational dimensions that families actually live with every day.

RCFE Owner & Operator Registered Behavior Technician B.A. Psychology — UC San Diego 20+ Years Dementia Care
Chapter Two

The story that made it personal.

For ten years, Gigi's grandmother — diagnosed with dementia — lived with her at home. Gigi was not just a professional during those years. She was a granddaughter, a full-time caregiver, and a person trying to hold everything together while watching someone she loved slowly change.

She knows the sleepless nights. The guilt about whether you're doing enough. The grief of losing someone in slow motion. The moments of unexpected connection that make it all worth it. And the point where home care becomes unsafe, and what it takes to make that call.

This is why Gigi consults differently. She's not just reciting information she learned in a textbook. She's been in your shoes — and she can sit with you in the hard moments without flinching, because she's already been there herself.

Chapter Three

Health Tech Advisor — where care meets innovation.

Alongside her work in eldercare, Gigi built a parallel career as a Health Tech Advisor — working at the intersection of technology, design, and aging. Affiliated with Caltech, ArtCenter College of Design, and JPL, she collaborated with scientists, designers, engineers, and investors to develop new ideas and startup concepts aimed at improving the lives of the elderly.

Her role wasn't theoretical. She worked directly with directors and investment fund managers, helping shape the direction of companies and innovations from the ground up — bringing her real-world RCFE experience into rooms where most eldercare voices were absent.

She consulted for Fortune 500 companies, traveled globally to collaborate with CEOs and industry leaders, and participated in CES (Consumer Electronics Show) for five consecutive years — one of the world's largest technology showcases — representing the intersection of tech and eldercare.

Chapter Four

Why a consulting practice — and why now.

After two decades of owning facilities, advising companies, collaborating with scientists, and caring personally for a family member with dementia — Gigi wanted to make her knowledge accessible to the people who need it most: families and caregivers navigating this journey without a guide.

The healthcare system is complicated. The eldercare industry is complicated. Benefits, facilities, transitions, behaviors, family dynamics — there is so much families don't know they don't know. Gigi built Ask Gigi to change that, one conversation at a time.

At $60 an hour, a session with Gigi costs less than most co-pays — and gives families something most co-pays don't: time, attention, real experience, and someone who has genuinely been on every side of this journey.

"Think of me as a knowledgeable friend who happens to have 20 years in the field, a psych degree, an RBT credential, and a seat at some of the most innovative tables in health tech. I'm not your doctor. But I can help you talk to yours — and make sure you're asking the right questions."

— Gigi Bratakos
